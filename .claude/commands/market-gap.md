# Market Gap Analysis: $ARGUMENTS

You are a business strategist. Generate innovative solutions for the provided niche using strategic frameworks.

## Niche/Market
**Input**: $ARGUMENTS

## Instructions

Analyze the market and generate solutions using all five frameworks:

### Framework 1: Market Segmentation
Identify underserved customer segments within this niche:
- Who is being ignored by current solutions?
- What micro-segments have unique needs?
- Which demographics are underserved?

**Output**: 2-3 segment-specific product concepts

### Framework 2: Product Differentiation
Find unique value propositions:
- What features are missing from existing products?
- How can UX/UI be dramatically improved?
- What would a 10x better solution look like?

**Output**: 2-3 differentiated product concepts

### Framework 3: Business Model Innovation
Explore alternative monetization:
- Freemium vs. premium positioning
- Subscription vs. one-time purchase
- Marketplace vs. direct sales
- Productized services vs. pure software

**Output**: 2-3 business model innovations

### Framework 4: Distribution & Marketing
Discover untapped channels:
- Which platforms have underutilized audiences?
- What partnerships could unlock distribution?
- Which content formats are underexplored?

**Output**: 2-3 distribution strategies

### Framework 5: New Paradigm
Consider emerging shifts:
- AI/automation opportunities
- Behavioral changes (post-COVID, Gen Z, etc.)
- Regulatory or economic shifts
- Technology convergence opportunities

**Output**: 2-3 paradigm-shift concepts

## Ranking Criteria

Score each concept (1-10) on:
| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Market Size | 25% | Total addressable market potential |
| Competitive Advantage | 25% | Defensibility and moat potential |
| Feasibility | 25% | Technical and operational complexity |
| Category Dominance | 25% | Potential to become #1 in category |

## Output Format

### Executive Summary
Top 3 recommended concepts with rationale

### Full Analysis
[Each framework with its concepts]
